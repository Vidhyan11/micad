Contribution 1

Faithful vs. fake explanations

Try it

Pick the high-risk case. Click Flip on Atypical pigment network. In Faithful-by-Concept the melanoma probability drops sharply — the concept genuinely drove the decision. Switch to the Leaky black-box and flip the same concept: the probability barely moves. It shows concepts but doesn’t use them — an unfaithful explanation that today’s methods never catch.

What we measure

  • Reliance — decision change when concepts are removed. Pure model 0.22; leaky ≈ 0.
  • Comprehensiveness — does removing the stated-important concepts change the call?
  • The gap between the two models is statistically significant (95% CI).

How the work is done

Under the hood

Cheap by design

A frozen dermatology foundation model turns each image into an embedding once. Only two tiny networks train: concepts → and concepts → diagnosis. Runs on a single free GPU.

The bottleneck is the point

The diagnosis head sees only the concept scores — never the raw image features. That is what makes the counterfactual test meaningful: the decision can only change through the concepts.
