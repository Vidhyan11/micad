\begin{abstract}
Dermatology AI increasingly claims to explain decisions through clinical
concepts---the 7-point checklist, ABCD---yet rarely tests whether those concepts
\emph{causally} drive the decision, and never whether such faithfulness is equitable
across skin tones. We present Faithful-by-Concept, a concept-bottleneck framework
that diagnoses through dermatologist concepts (bootstrapped from a frozen dermatology
foundation model), introduces a concept-counterfactual faithfulness test that
intervenes in concept space---no image generator---and audits whether faithfulness
holds across Fitzpatrick skin tones. On derm7pt, the bottleneck matches or exceeds an
image-only baseline for melanoma detection (AUROC 0.85 vs.\ 0.83) while remaining
interpretable. A common ``leaky'' concept model appears interpretable yet is causally
unfaithful (concept reliance $\approx 0$ vs.\ 0.22; significant), and we show
rank-correlation faithfulness is magnitude-blind. Reasoning is statistically
equitable across skin tones, and skin-tone-diverse training---not post-hoc
calibration---significantly improves dark-skin faithfulness. All experiments run on a
single GPU with frozen encoders.
\keywords{concept bottleneck, faithfulness, algorithmic fairness, dermoscopy,
explainable AI}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Concept-based explanations are attractive for clinical AI because they speak the
clinician's language: rather than a heatmap, the model reports \emph{why} in terms a
dermatologist already trusts---an atypical pigment network, a blue-whitish veil,
irregular streaks. A growing body of dermatology work routes predictions through such
concepts and presents the concept activations as an explanation of the diagnosis.

But a concept explanation is only worth trusting if it is \emph{faithful}---if the
concepts it cites are the ones that genuinely determine the decision---and only worth
deploying if that faithfulness is \emph{equitable} across the patient groups the model
will serve. Both properties are routinely assumed and almost never tested.
Concept-bottleneck papers assert faithfulness by architecture; fairness studies in
dermatology measure \emph{accuracy} gaps across skin tone but never gaps in
\emph{reasoning}. The result is a literature of models that look interpretable and
look fair, with neither claim verified.

We close both gaps with three contributions.
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{A concept-counterfactual faithfulness metric for dermatology.} Because
our diagnosis head consumes \emph{only} concepts, we intervene directly in concept
space---flip a concept from present to absent---and measure the causal effect on the
decision, with no generative model of images. We show that popular rank-correlation
faithfulness measures are magnitude-blind and can rate a concept-ignoring model as
faithful.
\item \textbf{Faithful-by-Concept}, a frozen-encoder concept bottleneck with concept
supervision bootstrapped from a dermatology foundation model where human labels do not
exist. Only two small heads are trained, and for the clinically-aligned detection task
it matches or beats an image-only model.
\item \textbf{The first fairness-of-reasoning audit} in dermatology AI: we measure
concept faithfulness, not just accuracy, across Fitzpatrick skin tones, and test a
group-conditional calibration mitigation---reporting an honest negative result.
\end{enumerate}
A leakage-clean protocol (lesion-level splits, embedding-based de-duplication)
underpins every result.

\section{Related Work}

\textbf{Concept-bottleneck and concept-alignment models.} Concept Bottleneck
Models~\cite{koh2020} predict human concepts then the label from them. In dermatology,
image--concept alignment~\cite{bie2024mica} and CLIP-based dermoscopic concept
predictors~\cite{patricio2024} provide concept explanations and test-time
intervention, but none \emph{tests} whether concepts are causally responsible, and
none examines fairness of the explanation.
\textbf{Post-hoc concept methods}~\cite{yuksekgonul2023} fit concept directions onto a
fixed classifier without certifying causal faithfulness.
\textbf{Foundation concept annotators} such as MONET~\cite{kim2024monet} score
concepts automatically; MONET is a labeling/auditing tool rather than a faithful
diagnostic system---we use this style of scoring to \emph{bootstrap} supervision.
\textbf{Counterfactual audits.} Pixel-level generative audits~\cite{degrave2023}
reveal shortcuts by editing images but need a generator and no concept vocabulary;
ours are inference-only in concept space.
\textbf{Faithfulness measurement} is long-studied~\cite{jacovi2020,adebayo2018};
ERASER~\cite{deyoung2020} operationalizes it via comprehensiveness and sufficiency,
which we adapt to concept space.
\textbf{Skin-tone fairness.} Daneshjou et al.~\cite{daneshjou2022} document accuracy
disparities across tone; conformal methods target fair coverage~\cite{lu2022}. All
concern accuracy or coverage---none measures whether \emph{reasoning} is equally
faithful across tones. To our knowledge, no prior work measures whether a skin-AI's
concept explanations are causally faithful, nor whether that faithfulness is equitable
across skin tones.

\subsection{Two-model, shared-metric design}
Dermoscopic 7-point concepts describe magnified sub-surface structure and are not
present in ordinary clinical photographs, so a single concept model cannot honestly
span both domains. We instantiate the framework twice, sharing one architecture and
one faithfulness metric. \emph{Model A (dermoscopic)} trains on derm7pt with expert
7-point concept ground truth and validates the faithfulness metric against trustworthy
concepts. \emph{Model B (clinical)} trains on Fitzpatrick17k clinical photographs with
clinical concepts (ABCD + visible morphology) bootstrapped from a foundation model,
and hosts the fairness audit.

\subsection{Concept bottleneck}
Figure~\ref{fig:arch} summarizes the architecture. A frozen encoder $f$
(DermLIP~\cite{dermlip2025}; DINOv2~\cite{oquab2024} as ablation)
maps an image $x$ to an embedding $z=f(x)$. A concept head $g$ predicts concept
probabilities $\hat c=\sigma(g(z))\in[0,1]^K$; a diagnosis head $h$ predicts the label
from concepts \emph{only}: $\hat y = h(\hat c)$. Only $g,h$ (small MLPs) are trained;
embeddings are extracted once. The purity of the bottleneck ($h$ never sees $z$) makes
the counterfactual test causally meaningful. We train sequentially with class-balanced
cross-entropy and balanced-accuracy model selection. Where concept ground truth is
absent, we score concepts zero-shot from the foundation model with an ensemble of
positive/negative text prompts and use the probabilities as soft supervision.

\subsection{Concept-counterfactual faithfulness}
For a case with concepts $\hat c$ and predicted class $\hat y$ we compare the model's
\emph{stated importance} $I_c$ (gradient of $P(\hat y)$ w.r.t.\ $\hat c_c$) with the
\emph{causal effect} $E_c$ of flipping $\hat c_c\!\to\!1-\hat c_c$ and re-running $h$.
We report \textbf{reliance} (decision-prob drop when all concepts are neutralized to a
reference), \textbf{comprehensiveness}/\textbf{sufficiency} (ERASER-style, using $I$ to
rank concepts), and \textbf{ccf\_corr} $=\mathrm{Spearman}(I,E)$. A central point:
ccf\_corr is \emph{magnitude-blind}---a model whose concepts are causally inert has
$I_c\!\approx\!0$ and $E_c\!\approx\!0$ yet can be perfectly rank-correlated.
Faithfulness must be judged by magnitude-aware measures, as we show empirically.

\subsection{Fairness of reasoning}
On Fitzpatrick17k we compute accuracy and faithfulness per skin-tone group (I--II,
III--IV, V--VI), report worst-vs-best gaps, and test the dark-vs-light difference with
a bootstrap. As a retraining-free mitigation we fit a per-group temperature on concept
logits~\cite{guo2017} on a held-out split. We also contrast light-skin-only vs.\
skin-tone-diverse training regimes.

\section{Datasets and Protocol}
\textbf{derm7pt}~\cite{kawahara2019}: 1{,}011 cases with expert 7-point concept
annotations; official 413/203/395 split; concepts binarized to the score-positive
variant. \textbf{Fitzpatrick17k}~\cite{groh2021}: 16{,}574 clinical images with
Fitzpatrick I--VI (V--VI: 2{,}168) and coarse benign/malignant/non-neoplastic labels;
no concept ground truth. \textbf{PAD-UFES-20}~\cite{pacheco2020}: 2{,}298 smartphone
lesions; its \texttt{elevation} field gives partial clinical concept ground truth for
validating the bootstrap. \textbf{Leakage-clean protocol}~\cite{cassidy2022}: near-duplicates removed by
high-similarity clustering on frozen embeddings ($\approx$600 from Fitzpatrick17k) and
group-aware, diagnosis-stratified splits. PH2 was excluded (no mirror ships images and
concept labels together).

\section{Experiments}
Encoder: DermLIP (frozen) unless noted; heads are 1-hidden-layer MLPs; 95\% CIs are
2{,}000-sample bootstraps.

\subsection{Faithfulness}
We compare the pure bottleneck against a \emph{leaky} CBM whose diagnosis head sees
concepts \emph{and} the embedding.

\begin{table}[t]
\caption{Concept-counterfactual faithfulness (95\% CI). Higher

\label{tab:exp2}
\begin{center}

\hline\rule{0pt}{12pt}
Model & Variant & reliance & compreh. & suffic. & ccf\_corr\\[2pt]
\hline\rule{0pt}{12pt}
A & pure bottleneck & \textbf{0.223} & \textbf{0.129} & 0.073 & 0.605\\
A & leaky & 0.000 & 0.000 & 0.000 & 0.819\\
B & pure bottleneck & \textbf{0.208} & \textbf{0.172} & 0.074 & 0.553\\

\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{table}

Table~\ref{tab:exp2} reports the results. The pure bottleneck's decisions genuinely
depend on its concepts (reliance 0.223
[0.212,0.236]) while the leaky model---displaying the same concepts---is causally inert
(reliance 0.000): flipping or erasing its concepts does not move the decision
(Fig.~\ref{fig:faith}). The pure$-$leaky gap is significant (Model A $\Delta=0.223$
[0.211,0.236]; Model B $\Delta=0.100$ [0.094,0.106]). Strikingly, the leaky model
scores \emph{higher} rank-agreement (ccf\_corr 0.819 vs.\ 0.605)---a concrete
demonstration that rank-correlation faithfulness is magnitude-blind and would certify
an unfaithful model.

Table~\ref{tab:exp3} reports the per-skin-tone audit.
\emph{(i) Reasoning is equitable within a regime}: the dark-vs-light faithfulness
difference is not significant (biased training, $\Delta_{\text{V--VI}-\text{I--II}}=
-0.003$ [$-0.016,0.009$], n.s.)---the first evidence that concept reasoning need not be
less faithful on dark skin. \emph{(ii) Data diversity is the effective lever}:
diverse training significantly improves dark-skin faithfulness over light-dominant
training (V--VI reliance $0.178\,[0.167,0.189]\!\to\!0.217\,[0.205,0.230]$;
comprehensiveness $0.139\!\to\!0.175$; non-overlapping CIs). \emph{(iii)} Group-
conditional calibration did \emph{not} significantly change the gaps (reliance gap
$0.019\!\to\!0.019$); we report this as a negative result. Effect sizes are modest,
consistent with the derm foundation encoder being partially skin-tone-robust.

\section{Discussion and Limitations}
Interpretability claims should be tested, not assumed, and the test is cheap when the
model is a genuine bottleneck. On fairness, reasoning faithfulness was equitable across
skin tones in every regime tested, and the actionable lever was training-data
diversity, not post-hoc calibration. Effect sizes are modest, partly because the derm
foundation encoder is already fairly skin-tone-robust. Clinical concept pseudo-labels
are noisy and only partially validated (PAD-UFES elevation, AUROC 0.63); we treat
pseudo-label quality as a studied variable. Our metric is defined within the
bottleneck; extending it to end-to-end models and multi-way diagnosis is future work.
Real 7-point concept ground truth exists at limited scale (derm7pt, $\sim$1k cases).

\section{Conclusion}
Faithful-by-Concept makes concept-based dermatology AI verifiable and lets us ask
whether it is equitable. It tests, rather than assumes, that the concepts an
explanation cites causally drive the diagnosis; shows that a widely-used class of
``interpretable'' models fails this test while a pure bottleneck passes it; and
delivers the first audit of whether that faithfulness is fair across skin tones,
finding reasoning equitable and data diversity---not calibration---to be the lever. The
framework is cheap, reproducible, and single-GPU.
