\begin{abstract}
Dermatology AI increasingly \emph{claims} to explain its decisions through clinical
concepts (the 7-point checklist, ABCD), yet almost no work tests whether those
concepts \emph{causally} drive the decision, and none asks whether such faithfulness
holds equally across skin tones. We present \textbf{Faithful-by-Concept}, a
concept-bottleneck framework that (i) diagnoses through dermatologist concepts with
supervision bootstrapped from a frozen dermatology foundation model, (ii) introduces
a \emph{concept-counterfactual faithfulness} test that flips a concept in concept
space and measures whether the decision changes as the explanation implies, and
(iii) performs the first \emph{fairness-of-reasoning} audit, testing whether concept
faithfulness degrades on darker (Fitzpatrick V--VI) skin, with a lightweight
group-conditional calibration mitigation. On derm7pt, our bottleneck matches or
exceeds an image-only baseline for melanoma detection (AUROC $0.852$ vs $0.830$)
while remaining fully interpretable, and we show that a common "leaky" concept model
appears interpretable yet is causally unfaithful (concept reliance $\approx 0$).
Under light-skin-dominant training, both accuracy and reasoning faithfulness degrade
on dark skin; skin-tone-diverse training restores both. All experiments run on a
single GPU with frozen encoders.
\keywords{Concept bottleneck \and Faithfulness \and Fairness \and Dermoscopy \and Explainable AI}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Concept-based explanations are attractive for clinical AI because they speak the
language of clinicians. But an explanation is only trustworthy if it is
\emph{faithful} --- if the concepts it cites are the ones that actually determine the
model's decision --- and only \emph{equitable} if that faithfulness holds across
patient groups. Current dermatology concept models assert faithfulness by
construction but never test it causally, and fairness work has measured accuracy gaps
(e.g.\ across skin tone) but never gaps in \emph{reasoning}.

We close both gaps. Our contributions are: (1) a \textbf{concept-counterfactual
faithfulness} metric for dermatology that intervenes directly in concept space ---
no image generator --- and measures whether the model's stated important concepts are
the ones that causally move the decision; (2) \textbf{Faithful-by-Concept}, a
frozen-encoder concept bottleneck whose diagnosis head sees \emph{only} concepts,
with concept supervision bootstrapped from a dermatology foundation model where
ground truth is unavailable; and (3) the first \textbf{fairness-of-reasoning audit}
across Fitzpatrick skin tones, plus a group-conditional calibration mitigation. A
leakage-clean protocol (lesion-level splits, embedding-based de-duplication)
underpins all results.

\section{Related Work}
Concept-bottleneck and image--concept alignment models (e.g.\ MICA, Patr\'icio et
al.) provide concept explanations but do not test causal faithfulness or fairness.
Post-hoc concept methods (Post-hoc CBM) attribute concepts to a fixed classifier.
Foundation concept annotators (MONET) score concepts but are not faithful diagnostic
systems. Generative counterfactual audits (DeGrave et al.) operate at the pixel level
and are expensive. Fairness studies (Daneshjou et al.; Lu et al.) address accuracy or
coverage gaps, not reasoning. \textbf{No prior work measures whether skin-AI concept
explanations are causally faithful, nor whether that faithfulness is fair across skin
tones} --- our white space.

\section{Method}
\subsection{Two-model, shared-metric design}
Dermoscopic 7-point concepts are not visible in clinical photographs, so we train two
concept bottlenecks sharing one architecture and one faithfulness metric.
\textbf{Model A} (dermoscopic) trains on derm7pt with real 7-point concept ground
truth and validates the faithfulness metric against trustworthy concepts.
\textbf{Model B} (clinical) trains on Fitzpatrick17k with foundation-bootstrapped
clinical concepts (ABCD + morphology) and hosts the fairness audit.

\subsection{Concept bottleneck}
A frozen encoder $f$ (DermLIP; DINOv2 as ablation) maps an image to an embedding
$z=f(x)$. A concept head $g$ predicts concept probabilities $\hat c=\sigma(g(z))\in
[0,1]^K$; a diagnosis head $h$ predicts the label from \emph{concepts only},
$\hat y=h(\hat c)$. Only $g,h$ (small MLPs) are trained. The pure bottleneck (h sees
only $\hat c$) is what makes the counterfactual test meaningful.

\subsection{Concept-counterfactual faithfulness}
For a case with concepts $\hat c$ and predicted class $\hat y$, we compare the model's
\emph{stated importance} $I_c$ (gradient of $P(\hat y)$ w.r.t.\ $\hat c_c$) with the
\emph{causal effect} $E_c$ of intervening on concept $c$ (flipping $\hat c_c
\rightarrow 1-\hat c_c$ and re-running $h$). We report: \emph{reliance} (decision-prob
drop when all concepts are neutralised to a reference), \emph{comprehensiveness} /
\emph{sufficiency} (ERASER-style, using $I$ to rank concepts), and a rank agreement
$\mathrm{ccf\_corr}=\mathrm{Spearman}(I,E)$. We show that rank agreement alone is
\emph{magnitude-blind} and must be paired with the magnitude-aware measures.

\subsection{Fairness of reasoning}
On Fitzpatrick17k we compute diagnosis accuracy \emph{and} faithfulness per skin-tone
group (I--II / III--IV / V--VI) and report worst-vs-best gaps. As a retraining-free
mitigation we fit a per-group temperature on concept logits (group-conditional
calibration) on a held-out split.

\section{Data and Protocol}
derm7pt ($1011$ cases; official $413/203/395$ split; real 7-point concept GT),
Fitzpatrick17k ($16{,}574$ images; Fitzpatrick I--VI; V--VI $=2168$), and PAD-UFES-20
($2298$ lesions; provides partial clinical GT --- elevation --- for validation). We
apply a leakage-clean protocol: group-aware stratified splits and embedding-based
near-duplicate removal (e.g.\ $\sim\!600$ Fitzpatrick17k near-duplicates dropped).

\section{Discussion and Limitations}
Foundation embeddings appear partially skin-tone-robust, so residual fairness gaps are
modest but consistently concentrate on dark skin under biased training. Clinical
concept pseudo-labels are noisy (validated where GT exists: PAD-UFES elevation AUROC
$0.63$); we treat pseudo-label quality as a studied variable. Absolute lesion size is
not visually recoverable from crops and was excluded.

\section{Conclusion}
Faithful-by-Concept makes concept explanations \emph{verifiable} and \emph{equitable}:
it tests, rather than assumes, that stated concepts causally drive diagnosis, and it
is the first to audit whether that faithfulness is fair across skin tones.
